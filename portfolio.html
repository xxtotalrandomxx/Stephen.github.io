Stephen Sum

 Artist Statement

Intro to Photoshop, Photoshop, 2019

This piece was meant to be an exercise to prepare people on the basics of using Photoshop. In this exercise, we were given separate images and combine them into an inanimate being with the skills learned in Photoshop.

Distorting Jellyfish, Photoshop, 2019

This piece was an exercise to practice editing in Photoshop. We mixed different filters and distortions in the software and messed around with the contrast and color of the image.

Hatsune Misfortu, Audio, 2019

This piece was my first attempt at distorting an image so that the image looks like it has a bug or “glitch” in the system while making the image. This piece gave me an opportunity to play around in an audio editing software and learn how to edit using sound waves.

Closet of Memories, Photoshop, 2019

This piece was a collaborative effort with a group of others and our idea was to combine our three pieces to connect as though these subjects of our choosing were stored away in a place where they would never be seen nor used again like in a closet or a hardware. My piece was a mix of a couple different popular animes that have been stored away as if they were kept as memories.

Pinky Finger, Photoshop, 2019

This piece was created to represent the making of current emojis in people’s media. I created a piece that resembled the same purpose as the finger emojis. I, instead, did the pinky finger sticking out because it means multiple purposes such as the etiquette of royalty drinking, or the swear finger in most asian cultures.

Unfortunate Life, Video, 2019

This piece was a video created of a story about this cup. It was unintentional at first, but as my partner and I took more and more photos, we found that this cup was in mostly all of our clips. When the video plays out, it tells a short preview of the adventure this cup has gone through before it meets its unfortunate demise.
